# Recruitment and Equal Opportunity Policy (Template)

**Company:** [Company Name]  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]  
**Owner:** HR Department  
**Applies to:** All hiring managers and recruitment participants

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how [Company Name] recruits and selects candidates fairly, transparently, and consistently, while supporting equal opportunity.

## 2. Equal opportunity commitment

Hiring decisions must be based on job-related criteria such as skills, experience, and role requirements.  
Discrimination and harassment are prohibited.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

1. HR: ensures process compliance, documentation, and candidate communication.
2. Hiring manager: defines role requirements, participates in interviews, makes the selection recommendation.
3. Interview panel: evaluates candidates using standardized criteria.

## 4. Recruitment process

### 4.1 Job definition and approval

1. Hiring manager drafts job description with required skills and responsibilities.
2. HR reviews for clarity and fairness.
3. Role is approved by [Department Head/Finance] where applicable.

### 4.2 Job posting

Job postings should include:

1. Role title and responsibilities
2. Required qualifications
3. Location and work mode (office/hybrid/remote)
4. Application deadline
5. Equal opportunity statement

### 4.3 Screening

HR and the hiring manager screen applications using a defined scorecard.  
Screening notes should be factual and job-related.

### 4.4 Interviewing

Interviews must:

1. Use consistent questions for comparable candidates.
2. Avoid collecting unnecessary personal data.
3. Focus on role competencies and practical scenarios.

### 4.5 Selection and offer

1. Hiring manager submits selection justification to HR.
2. HR verifies documentation completeness.
3. HR issues offer letter and contract steps.

## 5. Candidate data protection

Candidate data must be handled securely:

1. Stored only in approved systems.
2. Shared only with people involved in recruitment.
3. Retained only as long as necessary per company practice.

## 6. Conflicts of interest (nepotism)

If a candidate is a close relative or personal associate:

1. The hiring manager must disclose it to HR.
2. HR assigns alternative decision-makers where needed.

## 7. Complaints

Candidates or employees may raise concerns about recruitment fairness through HR.  
Retaliation is prohibited.
