# PTO and Leave Policy (Template)

**Company:** [Company Name]  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]  
**Owner:** HR Department  
**Applies to:** All employees (unless contract specifies otherwise)

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how employees request and use paid time off (PTO) and other types of leave at [Company Name]. It focuses on the internal process and approval workflow.

## 2. Scope and legal alignment

This policy is an internal procedure and must be applied in line with Kosovo’s applicable labour rules. If a conflict exists, legal requirements prevail.

## 3. Leave types covered

This policy covers:

1. Annual leave (PTO)
2. Sick leave
3. Special/exceptional leave (e.g., family events where applicable)
4. Unpaid leave (where approved)
5. Public holidays (per Kosovo official holiday rules)
6. Other leave types as defined by law/contract (e.g., parental leave)

## 4. Annual leave (PTO)

### 4.1 Eligibility

Annual leave applies to employees with an active employment contract.

### 4.2 Planning and scheduling

To ensure business continuity:

1. Teams should plan leave early when possible.
2. Managers may limit overlapping leave during critical periods.
3. HR may request leave plans during peak seasons.

### 4.3 Carryover of unused leave

Carryover rules depend on company decision and legal constraints.  
If not explicitly defined in this policy, the question should be treated as **“Not defined in company sources”** until HR updates this policy.

## 5. Sick leave

Employees who are unable to work due to illness must:

1. Notify the manager as soon as possible (preferably before the start of the workday).
2. Provide medical confirmation if required by law/contract/company rule.
3. Avoid working while unfit, especially where safety risks exist.

## 6. Request and approval process (standard)

### 6.1 How to request

Employees must submit leave requests through:

1. [HR System / Email / Form Name]
2. At least **7 calendar days** in advance for planned PTO (recommended).
3. As soon as possible for unplanned leave (e.g., illness).

### 6.2 Manager approval

Managers approve based on:

1. Team coverage and deadlines
2. Employee leave balance (if tracked)
3. Business needs
   Approval outcomes:
4. Approved
5. Approved with modified dates
6. Rejected with reason and alternative proposal

### 6.3 HR recording

Once approved, HR records the leave in:

1. [HR system / spreadsheet / payroll record]
2. Any required payroll or attendance system

## 7. Changes, cancellation, and early return

If the employee needs to change an approved leave:

1. Employee informs manager immediately.
2. Manager confirms whether the change is approved.
3. HR updates the record.

## 8. Public holidays

Public holidays are recognized according to Kosovo’s official holiday schedule.  
If the holiday schedule is not included in the company sources, HR will publish an annual calendar to employees.

## 9. Data protection

Medical or sensitive information related to leave must be handled confidentially.  
Only HR and authorized managers may access required leave documentation.

## 10. Contact

Questions about leave should be addressed to:

- HR: [HR Email / Contact]
- Manager: [Manager Name/Team Channel]
