# Remote and Hybrid Work Policy (Template)

**Company:** [Company Name]  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]  
**Owner:** HR + IT Security  
**Applies to:** Eligible roles as defined below

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the rules for remote and hybrid work at [Company Name], including eligibility, security requirements, and approval processes.

## 2. Definitions

1. Remote work: Working outside company office for one or more workdays.
2. Hybrid work: A mix of office days and remote days.
3. Remote location: A private workspace with stable internet, appropriate for professional work.

## 3. Eligibility

Eligibility depends on role requirements and performance expectations.  
Remote/hybrid work is not guaranteed and may be changed based on business needs.

## 4. Standard arrangement

Unless otherwise approved:

1. Hybrid arrangement: up to **2 remote days per week**
2. Office presence: required for key meetings, onboarding, client visits, or security needs

Managers may adjust arrangements depending on team requirements.

## 5. Approval process

1. Employee submits a request to their manager with desired schedule and location.
2. Manager reviews business impact and confirms approval or proposes changes.
3. HR records the arrangement (if required).
4. IT confirms security and access readiness (if required).

## 6. Working hours and availability

Employees must:

1. Follow agreed working hours.
2. Be reachable via [Teams/Slack/Email] during working hours.
3. Attend required meetings with camera/audio rules as defined by the team.

## 7. Information security (mandatory)

Remote work increases risk, so the following rules apply:

1. Do not share company devices with others.
2. Use strong passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where available.
3. Lock your screen when away.
4. Do not print or store confidential documents in unsecured places.
5. Use approved cloud storage and avoid personal email for company files.
6. Report suspicious activity immediately to IT/HR.

## 8. Equipment and expenses

1. Company equipment issued: [Laptop/Monitor/Other]
2. Employee is responsible for safe handling of issued equipment.
3. Expense reimbursement rules (internet/electricity) are defined by [Company Finance Policy] if available; otherwise they are “Not defined in sources”.

## 9. Health and safety

Employees must maintain a safe working environment.  
If an incident occurs during remote work:

1. Inform the manager as soon as possible.
2. HR records the incident and provides guidance.

## 10. Remote work outside Kosovo (cross-border)

Cross-border remote work can impact tax, insurance, and compliance.  
Therefore:

1. Any request to work from another country must be approved by HR and Finance.
2. If the required rules are not documented in company sources, the answer is treated as “Not found in sources” and escalated to HR/Finance.

## 11. Performance and review

Managers may review remote work arrangements based on:

1. Performance outcomes
2. Communication reliability
3. Security compliance
   Repeated violations may result in removing remote work privileges.
