## 1. Purpose

This handbook explains the basic working rules, expectations, and support processes at [Company Name]. It is designed to help employees understand how we work together, what standards we follow, and how to raise concerns safely.

## 2. Scope and hierarchy

This handbook is an internal policy document. It should be applied in line with applicable laws and regulations in the Republic of Kosovo. If there is a conflict between this handbook and applicable law, the legal requirement prevails.

## 3. Key principles

We aim to create a workplace that is respectful, safe, and fair.

1. Respect: We treat colleagues, clients, and partners professionally.
2. Integrity: We communicate honestly and avoid conflicts of interest.
3. Accountability: We do what we promise and protect company resources.
4. Confidentiality: We protect internal information and personal data.
5. Safety: We work in ways that reduce risk and prevent harm.

## 4. Employment basics

### 4.1 Employment relationship

Employment is governed by the employment contract and the applicable rules in Kosovo. Employees are expected to read and comply with company policies, including data protection and security rules.

### 4.2 Working hours and attendance

Managers define working hours and schedules per team needs. Employees must:

1. Start and end work on time as agreed.
2. Inform the manager as soon as possible if they will be late or absent.
3. Record time or attendance if required by the team process.

### 4.3 Workplace conduct

Employees must maintain professional behavior at all times. This includes:

1. No harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory behavior.
2. No violence, threats, or unsafe behavior.
3. No use of offensive language or repeated disrespectful conduct.
4. Compliance with internal security and access rules.

## 5. Equal treatment and respectful workplace

[Company Name] is committed to equal opportunity and a workplace free from discrimination.

1. Employment decisions must be based on job-related criteria.
2. Harassment and retaliation are prohibited.
3. Employees may request reasonable support or accommodations through HR, where applicable.

## 6. Confidentiality and information security

### 6.1 Confidential information

Confidential information includes business plans, client data, internal processes, source code, financial information, and any non-public materials.

### 6.2 Personal data protection

HR and managers will handle employee personal data only for legitimate HR purposes and according to company procedures.
Employees must not share personal data of colleagues or clients without authorization.

### 6.3 Acceptable use of company systems

Employees must use company equipment and accounts responsibly:

1. Do not share passwords or access tokens.
2. Do not install unapproved software on company devices.
3. Report suspected security incidents immediately.

## 7. Conflicts of interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests.
Examples include:

1. Working for a competitor without disclosure.
2. Hiring or supervising close relatives without HR approval.
3. Accepting gifts that could influence decisions.
   If unsure, employees should disclose the situation to HR.

## 8. Health, safety, and incident reporting

Employees must follow safety instructions and report hazards.
If an incident occurs:

1. Ensure immediate safety first.
2. Inform the manager as soon as possible.
3. HR documents the incident and coordinates next steps.

## 9. Reporting concerns

Employees can report concerns related to safety, harassment, discrimination, ethics, or policy violations.
Reporting options:

1. Direct manager (if appropriate)
2. HR contact: [HR Email / Contact]
3. Confidential channel (if available): [Channel]

Reports are handled with respect for confidentiality, and retaliation is prohibited.
