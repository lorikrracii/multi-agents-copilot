# Complaints and Disciplinary Procedure (Template)

**Company:** [Company Name]  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]  
**Owner:** HR Department  
**Applies to:** All employees

## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes how employees can raise concerns (complaints) and how [Company Name] investigates and resolves issues fairly. It also explains disciplinary actions when policies are violated.

## 2. What can be reported

Employees can report:

1. Harassment or discrimination
2. Bullying, intimidation, or threats
3. Safety concerns
4. Fraud, misconduct, or policy violations
5. Conflicts of interest
6. Data privacy or security concerns

## 3. Reporting channels

Employees can report concerns through:

1. Direct manager (if appropriate)
2. HR contact: [HR Email / Phone]
3. Confidential channel (if available): [Channel]

## 4. Anti-retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith is prohibited.  
Any retaliation should be reported immediately to HR.

## 5. Informal resolution (when appropriate)

Some issues can be resolved informally:

1. HR facilitates a discussion or coaching.
2. Manager sets clear expectations and timelines.
   If informal steps fail or the issue is serious, a formal investigation begins.

## 6. Formal investigation process

### 6.1 Intake and acknowledgment

1. HR acknowledges receipt of the complaint.
2. HR records the complaint details and assigns a case owner.

### 6.2 Fact finding

HR may:

1. Interview involved parties and witnesses.
2. Review relevant documents, messages, or system logs (only if authorized).
3. Collect evidence while respecting privacy and confidentiality.

### 6.3 Confidentiality

Information is shared only with those who need to know for the investigation.  
Absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed, but privacy is respected.

### 6.4 Outcome and actions

HR documents:

1. Findings (supported/not supported/inconclusive)
2. Actions (coaching, warnings, process changes, etc.)
3. Follow-up plan and deadlines

## 7. Disciplinary actions (principles)

Discipline is proportional to severity and may include:

1. Verbal warning (documented by manager/HR)
2. Written warning
3. Final written warning
4. Suspension (where applicable)
5. Termination (where applicable)

Serious misconduct may justify skipping steps.

## 8. Appeal process

Employees may request an appeal within **5 working days** of receiving a formal disciplinary decision.
HR reviews appeals with [Management/Legal] where applicable.

## 9. Data protection

Investigation notes and records contain sensitive information and must be stored securely with restricted access.
